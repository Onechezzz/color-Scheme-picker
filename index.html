Modes:

  • Random: Generates random colors for a vibrant and unpredictable palette.
  • Analogous Colors: Colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. They create a harmonious and soft effect.
  • Complementary Colors: Colors that are opposite on the color wheel. They are used to create contrast.
  • Triad: Three colors evenly distributed around the color wheel. Provide balance and contrast.
  • Split Complementary Colors: A variation of the complementary mode using adjacent colors instead of opposite ones.
  • Tetrad: Four colors, two pairs of opposite colors. Creates a rich color palette.
  • Monochromatic: Different shades of the same color. For minimalist and modern designs.
